Digital Ethnography

Twitter

Trouva’s posts consist of advertising sales and featured items as well as some ‘lifestyle’ tweets that link to blogs and events, or news related to Trouva themselves. Around 30% of the tweets directly @ one of the boutiques that sell on Trouva. Trouva’s Twitter account features a lot of images, with nearly all their posts featuring an image of an event or a particular item that they sell.

Trouva’s Twitter account is clearly aimed at customers, with the focus on advertisement of their brand and of products. No posts are directed towards engaging with sellers.

Engagement with Trouva’s posts is very low, with only a few posts in the last month receiving any likes or comments. Despite Trouva selling the products of over 400 stores, and cited as one of Europe’s top 5 fastest growing tech businesses, they have less than 7000 followers on Twitter, suggesting perhaps this platform is not their focus.

When looking at Trouva’s communication with other Twitter members there is no communication between Trouva and sellers, or sellers and customers. Trouva’s Twitter account seems to act mostly as a support channel for disgruntled customers.

Instagram

Trouva’s Instagram seems to be much more popular than their Twitter - with over 35,000 followers - and averaging 200-300 likes per post.

Their photos are almost all of products they sell on their site, but some of are storefronts. Both these image types are equally liked and commented on. The posts that generate the most engagement are those where Trouva runs a competition where the user must ‘follow’ the store that sells the product and mention a friend in the comments section of the photo - these types of posts attract comments in their hundreds or thousands as opposed to the average 5-30.

Conversation between Trouva and customers or sellers and customers is rare, echoing the lack of engagement seen on Twitter.

Facebook

Despite Trouva being ‘liked’ 40,000 times on Facebook, more than 4 times as much as on Twitter, the engagement levels are almost identical. Further to this, the posts on this platform are also almost identical with those posted on Twitter.

There is very little conversation between Trouva and customers, and apart from the odd mention of the boutiques that sell the items being advertised there is almost no sign of a seller presence at all.
